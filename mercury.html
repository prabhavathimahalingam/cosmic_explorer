Educational Content

Overview

Mercury is the smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System. It orbits the Sun every 88 days.

Surface

Mercury's surface is heavily cratered and similar in appearance to Earth's Moon.

Temperature

It has extreme temperature variations, ranging from -173°C at night to 427°C during the day.

Historical Timeline

1631 - First Observation

Pierre Gassendi makes the first recorded observation of Mercury through a telescope.

1974 - Mariner 10 Flyby

NASA's Mariner 10 becomes the first spacecraft to visit Mercury.

2008 - MESSENGER Mission

MESSENGER spacecraft performs multiple flybys and enters Mercury's orbit.

Space Missions

Mariner 10

The first spacecraft to visit Mercury, launched by NASA in 1973, performed flybys in 1974-75.

MESSENGER

Launched in 2004, it became the first spacecraft to orbit Mercury, studying its chemical composition and geology.

BepiColombo

A joint mission by ESA and JAXA, launched in 2018, currently en route to Mercury with arrival expected in 2025.

Historic Mercury Missions

1973-1975

Mariner 10

🇺🇸 NASA 🚀 Atlas SLV-3D 📡 Flyby Mission

First spacecraft to use gravity assist, captured 45% of Mercury's surface. Revealed cratered moon-like terrain and magnetic field.

2004-2015

MESSENGER

🇺🇸 NASA 🚀 Delta II 🛰 Orbiter

First orbital mission mapping 100% of Mercury. Discovered water ice in polar craters and organic compounds.

2018-Present

BepiColombo

🇪🇺 ESA/JAXA 🚀 Ariane 5 🛰 Dual Orbiter

Current joint ESA-JAXA mission studying magnetosphere and surface composition. Scheduled for Mercury orbit insertion in 2025.
