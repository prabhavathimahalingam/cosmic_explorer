Educational Content

Overview

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known astronomical object to harbor life.

Atmosphere

Earth's atmosphere is 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and 1% other gases, protecting life from harmful radiation.

Structure

Earth has a solid inner core, liquid outer core, mantle, and crust that forms the continents and ocean basins.

Biosphere

Earth supports millions of species including humans in ecosystems that form the biosphere.

Historical Timeline

1961 - First Human in Space

Yuri Gagarin becomes the first human to journey into outer space.

1969 - Moon Landing

Apollo 11 mission lands the first humans on the Moon.

1990 - Hubble Launch

Hubble Space Telescope is launched, revolutionizing our view of Earth from space.

2000 - ISS Becomes Operational

International Space Station begins continuous human presence in space.

2021 - Climate Change Focus

Increased global focus on Earth's climate and environmental changes.

Earth Observation Missions

Landsat Program

The longest-running enterprise for acquisition of satellite imagery of Earth, operational since 1972.

Sentinel Missions

Part of the European Copernicus Programme, providing radar and optical imaging for land and ocean monitoring.

GOES Series

Geostationary weather satellites providing continuous monitoring of Earth's weather systems.

Aqua/Terra

NASA satellites studying Earth's water cycle and climate systems.
