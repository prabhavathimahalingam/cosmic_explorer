Educational Content

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet in our solar system. Renowned for its deep blue color, it experiences supersonic winds and dynamic weather patterns, making it one of the most extreme worlds.

Historical Timeline

Key events in Neptune's exploration:

  • 1989: Voyager 2 flyby revealed Neptune's dynamic atmosphere and rings.
  • 1990: Hubble Space Telescope observations refined our view of its weather patterns.
  • 2000s: Ground-based telescopes and space observatories continue to study its storms.
