Educational Content

Overview

Venus is the second planet from the Sun, often called Earth's "sister planet" due to similar size and composition.

Atmosphere

Venus has a thick, toxic atmosphere of CO₂ with clouds of sulfuric acid, creating a strong greenhouse effect.

Surface

The surface is a volcanic landscape with mountains and valleys, but no liquid water due to extreme temperatures.

Rotation

Venus rotates very slowly (243 Earth days) and in the opposite direction to most planets (retrograde rotation).

Historical Timeline

1962 - Mariner 2 Flyby

First successful interplanetary mission, confirming Venus's extreme surface temperatures.

1975 - Venera 9 Landing

First spacecraft to transmit images from Venus's surface, surviving for 53 minutes.

1990-1994 - Magellan Mission

Mapped 98% of Venus's surface with radar, revealing volcanoes and impact craters.

2015 - Akatsuki Orbit

Japanese orbiter studying Venus's atmosphere and weather patterns.

Space Missions

Venera Program

Soviet missions (1961-1984) that included the first successful landing and photos from Venus's surface.

Pioneer Venus

NASA missions (1978) that studied Venus's atmosphere and mapped its surface.

Magellan

NASA orbiter (1990-1994) that created detailed radar maps of Venus's surface.

Akatsuki

JAXA orbiter (2015-present) studying Venus's super-rotating atmosphere.
